This page shows alleles that have two or more classification records associated with them - where at least one of the classification records belongs to a lab you are part of.

This page can be a useful tool see how your lab has classified a variant in comparison to another lab/s or to detect duplicate entries from your lab.

Two (or more) classification records are considered discordant if they meet all of the following criteria:

  • Belong to the same allele
  • Belong to the same clinical group within that allele (including the “default” clinical group)
  • Are shared at the Shariant Users level or 3rd Party Database level
  • Have classifications that fall into two or more of the following buckets:
    • Benign/Likely Benign
    • VUS (A/B/C)
    • Likely Pathogenic/Pathogenic
    • Risk Factor
    e.g. Likely Benign is discordant with VUS, but Likely Benign is concordant with Benign.
